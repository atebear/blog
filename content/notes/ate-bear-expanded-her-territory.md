***Building My Backup Site***

If you visit [my Now page](/now), you’ll notice that I checked off one of my goals: **Have a backup site for my BearBlog**.

You may ask, “Why?”

Why would I need a backup website?

Hear me out.

Although [BearBlog.dev](https://bearblog.dev) is free, some of its features are not, so there are limitations on what you can do.

If you don’t know, I’m actually a web developer in real life — not just a blogger 😅

And just like [Herman](https://herman.bearblog.com), the person behind [BearBlog](https://bearblog.dev), I’ve tried a bunch of other blogging platforms before: [WordPress](https://wordpress.com), [Blogspot](https://blogspot.com), [Tumblr](https://tumblr.com), [Wix](https://wix.com), [Medium](https://medium.com), and many more.

Honestly, I was a blogger before I learned how to code.

And when I eventually learned how to build websites, <mark>I became obsessed with finding ways to create websites completely free — with no ads and no unnecessary clutter whatsoever</mark>.

I finally achieved that in 2023 🎉 when I discovered static site generators like [Hugo](https://gohugo.io) and [Hexo](https://hexo.com).

> 💡 Fun fact:\
> [BearBlog](https://bearblog.dev) actually has a Hugo theme version called [Hugo-BearBlog](https://github.com/janraasch/hugo-bearblog)!

So technically, with [GitHub](https://github.com), [Hugo](https://gohugo.io), and the [Hugo-BearBlog theme](https://github.com/janraasch/hugo-bearblog), I can create a BearBlog-style website completely from scratch.

<mark>No analytics. No unnecessary extras. Just simple blogging.</mark>

Not all of [BearBlog.dev](https://bearblog.dev)’s features are there, of course, but it’s definitely doable. I can manage 😌

If you want to see my backup site, you can visit [atebear.pages.dev](https://atebear.pages.dev)

There’s still a lot of work to do there, though. I’m still learning and slowly improving it little by little.
